# Poster Plan

## Format

The poster is presented digitally — no printing needed. It should be treated as a business pitch to stakeholders. The goal is to persuade someone to buy or invest in the system by showing its value, its users, and the evidence behind the design decisions.

Poster pitch is 5 minutes. Keep it tight — one person can lead or split it across two people.

---

## Suggested Sections

### 1. Headline and Tagline

Something short that says what the system is and who it is for.

Example: "EAJO Air — A flight booking platform built around real passengers"

---

### 2. The Problem

What is wrong with current flight booking systems? Use evidence from the user interviews to make this real.

Key quotes to use:
- "If they'd texted me two hours earlier I could have sorted it from home." — Participant A (Maddie)
- "My youngest ended up four rows away from us. She was five." — Participant B (Ben)
- "I've had passengers tell me themselves 'I'm a gold member' — I have to go hunting to confirm it." — Participant C (Cara)

Keep this to 2-3 bullet points or quotes. The quotes are stronger than a written paragraph.

---

### 3. Who It's For

Show the four personas briefly — a name, role, and one-line goal for each.

| Persona | Role | What they need |
|---|---|---|
| Marcus Chen | Business Traveller | Fast booking with schedule filters |
| David O'Brien | Family Traveller | Adjacent seats for 5, no surprises |
| Priya Sharma | Airline Agent | One screen with all passenger info |
| James Bailey | Flight Analyst | Live booking data and charts |

---

### 4. Key Features

Pick the 4-5 most visually impressive or differentiated features. Each one should connect back to a user need.

Suggested features to highlight:
- Flight search with price, time, and stopover filters — addresses Marcus and Maddie's pain point
- Interactive seat map with colour coding and family-adjacent selection — addresses David and Ben
- Admin dashboard with live charts (bookings over time, busiest routes, status breakdown) — addresses James
- Passenger lookup and frequent flyer visibility for staff — addresses Priya and Cara
- Automated SMS-style notifications for delays and cancellations — addresses Ashley's user story

Include a screenshot of the seat map and the admin dashboard — these are the most visually distinctive parts of the system.

---

### 5. How We Know It Works

Evidence that the system delivers real value:
- 3 user interviews conducted — specific pain points identified and addressed
- 79 automated tests all passing
- WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility audit completed
- CI/CD pipeline — every change tested automatically before merging

---

### 6. Tech Stack (brief)

Keep this small — one column or a row of logos/labels.

Kotlin + Ktor, SQLite, Pebble Templates, Gradle, GitHub Actions

---

## Pitch Script (5 minutes)

Suggested split — one person does problem and users, the other does features and evidence.

Person 1 (2 minutes):
- Introduce the product in one sentence
- Explain the problem using the three interview quotes
- Briefly introduce the four personas

Person 2 (3 minutes):
- Walk through the key features on screen, pointing at the poster
- Show the seat map screenshot and explain why it matters
- Show the admin dashboard and explain the analyst use case
- Close with the evidence section — interviews, tests, WCAG, CI/CD

---

## Design Tips

- Use the navy and gold colour scheme from the app so the poster feels like part of the product
- Lead with the quotes — they are the most compelling part of the research
- Screenshots should be large enough to read on a big screen
- Avoid walls of text — bullet points and visuals only
- The marker is looking for: benefits, key features, value to users, and evidence of value
